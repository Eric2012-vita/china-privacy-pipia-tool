DPO Assessment

Is the reviewed vendor-style DPA a good DPA? As a reference, yes: it covers the core GDPR Article 28 architecture, including controller/processor roles, documented instructions, confidentiality, subprocessors, security, audits, data-subject assistance, breach notification, international transfers, and deletion or return.

Is it a good public template as-is? No. It is written for one vendor's services, support channels, affiliates, security documents, subprocessor notice process, and product limits. A public template should be neutral, fillable, and balanced between controller and processor.

Important drafting point: Do not publish a company-specific DPA as a template. Replace names, product references, support portals, external policy links, affiliate definitions, and one-sided operational limits with neutral placeholders and actual annexes.

Recommended Checkpoints

Roles are clearIdentify the controller, processor, and any joint-controller or independent-controller activities.
Annex 1 is completeDescribe subject matter, duration, purposes, categories of data, data subjects, locations, and retention.
Security is specificAttach real technical and organizational measures, not only a link to a changing policy page.
Subprocessors are manageableUse general authorization only with notice, objection rights, onward obligations, and processor liability.
Breach timing protects the 72-hour clockUse "without undue delay" plus a practical outside deadline, often 24, 48, or 72 hours depending on risk.
Transfers are not vagueChoose SCC module, UK Addendum, transfer impact assessment, and supplementary measures where needed.
Audit rights are balancedAllow reports first where reasonable, but preserve inspection rights for material risk or regulator demand.
Deletion is provableRequire return/deletion at termination, backup deletion schedule, and certification on request.

Data Processing Agreement Template

Effective date: [Date]

Controller: [Controller legal name]

Processor: [Processor legal name]

Related agreement: [Service agreement / order form / master agreement]

1. Purpose and Scope

1.1 This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the related agreement between the parties and applies where Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of Controller in connection with the services described in Annex 1.

1.2 If this DPA conflicts with the related agreement, this DPA controls for the processing of Personal Data, unless the related agreement provides more protective data-protection terms.

1.3 Capitalized terms not defined in this DPA have the meanings given in applicable Data Protection Laws.

2. Roles of the Parties

2.1 The parties agree that Controller is the controller and Processor is the processor for the processing described in Annex 1.

2.2 Each party will comply with Data Protection Laws that apply to its role. Controller is responsible for the lawfulness of the processing instructions and for providing any required notices to data subjects.

2.3 Processor will process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller, including instructions in this DPA, the related agreement, order forms, and Controller's authorized configuration of the services.

3. Documented Instructions

3.1 Processor will not process Personal Data for its own purposes except where required by law or expressly authorized in writing by Controller.

3.2 Processor will promptly inform Controller if, in Processor's opinion, an instruction infringes Data Protection Laws, unless prohibited by law from doing so.

3.3 Any change request that materially changes the scope, risk, or cost of processing should be documented by the parties before implementation.

3.4 Processor will not use Personal Data for advertising, profiling, product improvement, AI training, model evaluation, or analytics unrelated to the services unless Controller has expressly authorized that use in Annex 1 or a signed amendment.

4. Confidentiality

4.1 Processor will ensure that persons authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations or are subject to an appropriate statutory duty of confidentiality.

4.2 Processor will limit access to Personal Data to personnel and subprocessors who need access to provide the services and who are subject to appropriate access controls.

5. Security Measures

5.1 Processor will implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or unauthorized access.

5.2 The minimum security measures are described in Annex 2. Processor will not materially decrease those measures during the term.

5.3 The parties should review Annex 2 where processing involves sensitive data, large-scale monitoring, children, health data, financial data, precise location, employee data, or regulated industry data.

6. Subprocessors

6.1 Controller gives Processor general written authorization to engage subprocessors listed in Annex 3, subject to this section.

6.2 Processor will give Controller prior notice of intended new or replacement subprocessors by [email / dashboard / written notice] at least [30] days before the change, unless urgency or security requires a shorter period.

6.3 Controller may object on reasonable data-protection grounds. The parties will work in good faith to resolve the objection, including through additional safeguards, alternative processing arrangements, or termination of the affected services.

6.4 Processor will impose data-protection obligations on each subprocessor that are no less protective than those in this DPA. Processor remains responsible for each subprocessor's performance of those obligations.

7. Assistance With Data Subject Requests

7.1 Taking into account the nature of the processing, Processor will assist Controller by appropriate technical and organizational measures to respond to data-subject requests under Data Protection Laws.

7.2 If Processor receives a request directly from a data subject concerning Controller's Personal Data, Processor will not respond substantively unless authorized by Controller or required by law. Processor will direct the data subject to Controller where appropriate.

8. Personal Data Breach

8.1 Processor will notify Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Personal Data processed on behalf of Controller, and in any event within [24 / 48 / 72] hours unless a shorter deadline is required by law or the related agreement.

8.2 Processor's notice will include, to the extent known, the nature of the breach, affected systems, categories and approximate number of data subjects and records, likely consequences, measures taken or proposed, and a contact point for follow-up.

8.3 Processor will take reasonable steps to contain, investigate, mitigate, and remediate the breach and will provide Controller with updates as information becomes available.

8.4 Processor will not make public statements about a breach involving Controller's Personal Data without Controller's prior written approval, unless required by law.

9. DPIA, Prior Consultation, and Compliance Assistance

9.1 Processor will provide reasonable information and assistance necessary for Controller to conduct data protection impact assessments, prior consultation with supervisory authorities, records of processing, and other controller compliance duties.

9.2 The parties should agree whether assistance is included in service fees or billed separately when requests are unusually extensive, urgent, or outside normal service scope.

10. Audit and Information Rights

10.1 Processor will make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA.

10.2 Where available, Processor may provide current independent audit reports, certifications, security summaries, or compliance reports as the first method of audit response.

10.3 Controller may conduct or appoint an independent auditor to conduct an audit where reports are insufficient, a material incident has occurred, a regulator requires it, or there is a reasonable basis to believe Processor is not complying with this DPA.

10.4 Audits must be conducted during normal business hours, with reasonable notice, under confidentiality obligations, and in a manner that does not unreasonably disrupt Processor's business or compromise other customers' data.

11. International Transfers

11.1 Processor will not transfer Personal Data outside the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland unless a valid transfer mechanism is in place, as described in Annex 4.

11.2 Where Standard Contractual Clauses, the UK Addendum, or another transfer tool is required, the parties will complete the relevant module, append it to this DPA, and apply supplementary measures where required by the transfer risk assessment.

11.3 Processor will promptly notify Controller if it can no longer comply with the applicable transfer mechanism.

12. Government and Legal Access Requests

12.1 Processor will notify Controller of any legally binding request for access to Personal Data by a public authority unless prohibited by law.

12.2 Processor will review each request for legal validity, resist unlawful or overbroad requests where legally permitted, and disclose only the minimum Personal Data required.

13. Return or Deletion

13.1 At the end of services, Processor will, at Controller's choice, return or delete Personal Data unless retention is required by law.

13.2 Processor will delete remaining copies from active systems within [30 / 60 / 90] days and from backups according to the backup deletion cycle stated in Annex 2.

13.3 On request, Processor will provide written confirmation of deletion or return.

14. Sensitive and Regulated Data

14.1 Controller will not provide sensitive, special-category, criminal-offence, health, financial, children's, biometric, precise-location, or other regulated data unless Annex 1 identifies that data and Annex 2 includes safeguards appropriate to the risk.

14.2 If such data is introduced after signature, the parties should update Annex 1 and Annex 2 before processing begins.

15. Term and Survival

15.1 This DPA remains in effect for as long as Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

15.2 Confidentiality, deletion, audit evidence, transfer, and liability-related provisions survive as necessary to protect Personal Data and enforce this DPA.

16. Definitions

Data Protection Laws means all privacy, data-protection, and data-security laws applicable to the processing under this DPA, including the GDPR and UK GDPR where applicable.

Personal Data, processing, controller, processor, data subject, and supervisory authority have the meanings given in applicable Data Protection Laws.

Personal Data Breach means a breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data processed by Processor on behalf of Controller.

17. Liability and Governing Terms

17.1 Liability, indemnity, governing law, venue, and payment terms are governed by the related agreement unless mandatory Data Protection Laws require otherwise.

17.2 Nothing in this DPA limits either party's obligation to comply with Data Protection Laws or the enforceability of any applicable Standard Contractual Clauses.

Annex 2: Technical and Organizational Measures

  • Access controls, least privilege, role-based permissions, and periodic access review.
  • Encryption in transit and, where appropriate, encryption at rest.
  • Logging, monitoring, vulnerability management, patching, and malware protection.
  • Secure development, change management, and production access controls.
  • Backup, business continuity, disaster recovery, and restoration testing.
  • Personnel confidentiality, security training, and acceptable-use controls.
  • Incident response process, escalation path, and breach notification workflow.
  • Physical and environmental security for facilities used to process Personal Data.
  • Data segregation, tenant isolation, pseudonymization, or anonymization where appropriate.
  • Deletion, return, and media disposal procedures.

Annex 5: Optional Jurisdiction-Specific Terms

Add terms for UK GDPR, Swiss FADP, US state privacy laws, China PIPL entrusted processing, or sector rules only where they actually apply. Keep those terms separate so the core GDPR DPA remains readable.
